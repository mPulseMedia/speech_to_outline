SPEC 01 PARAGRAPH

1.  Feature
    A.  Stream → paragraph formation.

2.  Streaming display
    A.  New text appears immediately.
    B.  New text appends to the end of the current (open)
        paragraph.
    C.  There is exactly one open paragraph at a time (the
        last one).

3.  Cleave (split) rule
    A.  When a boundary becomes clear, split the open
        paragraph into:
        1.  Front: becomes a closed paragraph.
        2.  Tail: becomes the new open paragraph (continue
            appending).

4.  Recognition vs boundary location
    A.  Boundary marker: marks where the split belongs in the
        text.
    B.  Recognition marker: marks when the system decides a
        split exists.
        1.  Recognition → boundary link: the recognition
            marker must point to the boundary marker it
            identifies.
        2.  Recognition happens after the stream passes what
            will become the boundary.
        3.  Marks used in the app (fields on the new
            paragraph/thought):
            a.  Boundary marker:
                `boundaryAfterUtteranceId`
            b.  Recognition marker:
                `boundaryRecognizedAtUtteranceId`
            c.  Recognition → boundary link:
                `boundaryRecognizedForUtteranceId`
        4.  Example (IDs are illustrative):
            Boundary marker: after utterance `u_123` (end of
            paragraph 1).
            Recognition marker: at utterance `u_129` (when it
            became clear).

5.  Boundary signals (apply conservatively)
    A.  Scripted: `@@thought:` forces a new paragraph boundary.
    B.  Non-markup: blank lines create paragraph boundaries.
    C.  Live speech: long pause can force a boundary
        (`pauseBoundaryMs`).
    D.  Fallback: split only with enough substance + low
        cohesion; cap growth.
